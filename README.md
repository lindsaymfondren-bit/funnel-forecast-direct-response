# Funnel Forecast | Direct Response

Forecast what your paid traffic spend could produce across common direct-response funnels.

## What this is
This is a lightweight forecasting tool for planning common direct-response funnels, including:
- Lead Magnet
- Tripwire
- Digital Product
- Webinar
- VSL / Call Booking

It helps estimate likely leads, sales, Return on Ad Spend (ROAS), break-even points, and rough profitability based on your assumptions.

## Who this is for
This tool is useful for marketers, offer owners, media buyers, and business owners who want to pressure-test funnel economics before spending money.

## How to use it
1. Choose a funnel type.
2. Enter your assumptions for ad spend, traffic cost, conversion rates, pricing, and optional expenses.
3. Review the results to estimate lead volume, sales, revenue, break-even targets, and overall viability.

Use the same forecast period throughout: weekly, monthly, quarterly, or another consistent time frame.

## Important notes
- This is a planning tool, not a guarantee.
- The quality of the forecast depends on the quality of your assumptions.
- Use your real past metrics when possible.
- Benchmark numbers are directional starting points, not universal truth.
- Cold traffic usually performs differently than warm traffic.

## Channels
This tool is designed for paid traffic forecasting and can be used with channels such as Meta, Google, LinkedIn, and more when you have reasonable funnel assumptions.

## What to watch
- Traffic costs can rise during competitive times of year.
- Click costs can increase when more advertisers compete for the same audience.
- A funnel can generate sales and still not work financially if margins are too weak after ad spend and expenses.
- Tripwire funnels may break even or run at a small front-end loss while creating buyers for later offers.

## Benchmark notes
Benchmarks included in the tool are based on reputable published sources and general industry references. They should be treated as starting points, then adjusted based on your actual funnel, audience, season, and offer.
